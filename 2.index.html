history

Tekirdağ's ancient history is parallel to other provinces in Thrace; The region, which was first inhabited by the Thracians, was conquered by Murat I in 1357 and joined the Ottoman lands, following Macedonian, Persian, Roman and Byzantine sources. Tekirdağ, which also hosted the rebellious Hungarian prince Rakoczi, who fought against the Austrian Empire in 1703, was a sanjak center attached to the Edirne province at the end of the Ottoman period. The province, which was occupied by the Russians in the 93 War (1878), by the Bulgarians in the Balkan War (1912) and by the Greeks after the First World War (1920-1922), was permanently occupied by the Turkish lands on 13 November 1922 after the victory in the Turkish War of Independence. joined.

natural beauties

Tekirdağ, where blue and green are intertwined; It is a travel destination with magnificent natural beauties with the views of the Marmara Sea on one side and Ganos Mountain on the other. Trekking sports tours are quite developed in Tekirdağ, which attracts attention especially from those who love to hike. The city's trekking routes attract visitors from all over the world. Tekirdağ, where camping and caravan tourism develops in nature, is ideal for spending a few days alone with nature.
